Master Loop — walkthrough (07)

This chart is the outer skeleton of a battle: how battle rounds, player turns, and scoring steps repeat until the game ends. Read it top to bottom; yellow diamonds are forks that loop back or exit.

  1. Start of Battle Round (green hexagon) — A new battle round begins. Battle Round 1 starts here with no prior end-of-round step. From round 2 onward, you arrive here only after the previous round’s End of Battle Round step has finished.
  2. Resolve Army/Faction Rules — Both players apply army-wide or faction rules that trigger at round start (e.g. Oaths of Moment, Strands of Fate). These sit outside the core phase sequence but must be resolved before anyone takes a turn.
  3. Start of Turn Step — The active player resolves all start-of-turn abilities and mission rules that fire when a player is about to act (07.02).
  4. Active Player Turn — The active player runs the full turn in order: Command → Movement → Shooting → Charge → Fight. Each phase has its own tab and flowchart in this reference.
  5. End of Player Turn — Resolve end-of-turn rules and mission scoring for this player’s turn (07.04). OC/VP may also be checked at end of each phase during the turn (14.04).
  6. Has the opposing player taken their turn? (yellow diamond)
    No — The opponent has not yet acted this round. Loop back to Start of Turn Step with the other player as active. Player 1 always starts Battle Round 1; turns strictly alternate within a round.
    Yes — Both players have completed one turn. Proceed to end the battle round.
  7. End of Battle Round (green hexagon) — Both turns are done. Resolve end-of-round rules and score objectives again (07.03, 14.04).
  8. Have 5 battle rounds been completed? (yellow diamond) — Default core length; missions may specify a different number.
    No — Start the next battle round at the top hexagon.
    Yes — The battle ends.
  9. End Battle & Determine Victor — Apply mission tie-breakers and declare the winner.

Key distinction: End of Turn happens twice per round (once per player). End of Battle Round happens once, after both players have finished, and triggers additional scoring.

Common Mistakes

  • Coherency: requires 2"/5" to one other model and 9"/5" to every other — not a "7+ models" rule.
  • HEAVY: +1 hit only if unengaged, not set up this turn, and no model moved >3" (Advancing counts as moving).
  • Fire Overwatch: end of opponent's Movement phase only; uses Snap Shooting.
  • Scoring: OC checked at end of each phase, end of turn, and end of battle round.
  • Crushing Impact: used just after each MON/Veh charge move — not at end of Charge phase.
  • Engaging consolidation: newly engaged enemies that have not fought this phase must be selected to fight now.
  • Melee weapons: each model selects one melee weapon when fighting (split attacks across targets, not weapons).
  • Indirect / CQ shooting: unit must not have Advanced this turn.
  • Pile-in / consolidate: active player resolves all their units first; models in base contact cannot move.

Command Phase — walkthrough (08)

The Command phase is linear: no loops inside the chart. The active player resolves battle-shock tests; CP gain affects both players at the same moment.

  1. Start of Command Phase — The active player’s turn enters its first phase. Stratagems and abilities with “start of Command phase” timing may be used now.
  2. Gain Core CP (yellow) — Both players gain 1CP, regardless of who is active (08.02). This happens before battle-shock tests.
  3. Battle-Shock Step (red hexagon) — The active player must take a battle-shock test for every unit that is at or below half-strength or already battle-shocked (order chosen by active player). Each rolls 2D6 vs Leadership. Pass removes battle-shocked status; fail applies battle-shocked (OC becomes ‘−’, no stratagem targeting, actions fail) (08.03).
  4. Command Abilities — Resolve any command-phase abilities, aura updates, or stratagems that trigger here (e.g. Insane Bravery is declared just before a test, not in this box, but other army rules land here).
  5. End of Command Phase — Resolve end-of-phase rules, then mission scoring if triggered (08.05, 14.04). Transition to Movement phase.

Movement Phase — walkthrough (09)

The Movement phase is a unit loop: the active player picks one eligible unit at a time until every unit that will move has been resolved—or skipped.

  1. Start of Movement Phase — Phase begins; end-of-phase scoring may be checked after the phase completes (14.04).
  2. Select Unit to Move — Active player chooses an unengaged unit (or an engaged unit for fall-back), or a transport/passenger for disembark/embark sequences. Units already resolved this phase are skipped on later passes.
  3. Move Type (purple hexagon) — Declare exactly one mode for that unit:
    • Remain Stationary — 0" move; can enable [HEAVY] later if other conditions met.
    • Normal Move — Up to M", must end unengaged.
    • Advance — Up to M + D6"; cannot charge or start actions; may Assault shoot (09.04).
    • Fall-back — Ordered retreat (optional if not battle-shocked) or Desperate Escape (mandatory if battle-shocked) (09.05).
    • Embark — During a Normal/Advance move: unit wholly within 3" of transport, not set up this turn (18.02).
    • Disembark — Passengers leaving a transport (Rapid / Tactical / Combat modes) (18.03).
    • Ingress — Strategic Reserves units arriving on the battlefield (20) — distinct from disembark.
  4. Constraints (yellow) — Apply coherency (2"/5" to one model, 9"/5" to all others), terrain, and engagement rules. Desperate Escape / Combat Disembark: make hazard rolls before moving each model (09.07, 18.03).
  5. More units? (yellow diamond)
    Yes — Return to Select Unit to Move for the next unit.
    No — Every unit has been moved or declared stationary; the phase ends.
  6. End of Movement Phase — Opponent may use Fire Overwatch and Rapid Ingress at end of this phase before Shooting begins (15.08, 15.07).

Shooting Phase — walkthrough (10)

The first chart above is the unit loop for shooting. Each iteration fires one eligible unit completely before the next is selected.

  1. Start of Shooting Phase — Phase opens. Smokescreen may be declared at start of opponent’s Shooting phase (15.10).
  2. Select Eligible Unit — Pick a unit on the battlefield that has not already shot this phase. Battle-shocked units can still be selected (they may affect actions/eligibility elsewhere even if they make no attacks). Units that Advanced or fell back cannot shoot (except Assault after Advance).
  3. Shooting Type (purple hexagon) — Determine which mode applies based on what the unit did this turn:
    • Normal — Unengaged, did not Advance.
    • Assault — Advanced; only [ASSAULT] weapons.
    • Close-Quarters — Engaged, did not Advance; CQ/PISTOL at engaged enemies.
    • Indirect — Unengaged, did not Advance; [INDIRECT FIRE] weapons may target without LoS (other weapons still need visible targets) (10.07).
  4. Select Weapons — Choose which ranged weapons fire. Identical weapons may be combined. Each weapon profile picks one target unit.
  5. Select Targets (pink) — For each weapon, run the Target Selection decision tree (second chart above). Invalid targets cannot be selected.
  6. Resolve Attacks (red) — For each targeted enemy unit: gather attack dice (batch identical weapons) → Hit → Wound → Save → Damage (loop until all weapons resolved) (04.03, 04–06). Resolve [HAZARDOUS] after the unit finishes shooting.
  7. More units? (yellow diamond)
    Yes — Loop to Select Eligible Unit.
    No — End of Shooting Phase.

Target Selection — walkthrough (06.01, 10, 13, 24.24)

The second chart above runs once per weapon when choosing a target. Follow each diamond in order; any “No” on a hard gate sends you to Cannot select target (pink). A path reaching Valid target (green) proceeds to hit modifiers, then the attack sequence.

  1. Pick candidate enemy unit — Start with a unit you intend to shoot with this weapon.
  2. Within weapon range? — Every model firing the weapon must be in range of at least one model in the target unit (unless Indirect bypasses LoS, not range).
  3. Shooting type eligible? — Normal/Assault/CQ/Indirect rules must allow this unit state and weapon combination (10.03–10.07).
  4. Engaged targeting OK? — Normal and Assault cannot target engaged units; Close-Quarters only engaged enemies; [BLAST] never targets engaged units (even MON/Veh CQ) (10.06, FAQ).
  5. Lone Operative — target within 12"? — Unattached Characters/Agents cannot be selected beyond 12" by ranged attacks (24.24).
  6. Draw Line of Sight — From any firing model to any target model, 1 mm line; ignore models in the firing unit (06.01). Then check terrain in sequence:
    • Solid — Dense terrain gap ≤3" from ground blocks LoS (13.11).
    • Obscuring — If LoS crosses a light/dense area and neither model is inside, target is NOT VISIBLE (13.10).
    • Hidden — INF/B/S in dense terrain that has not shot this or the previous turn: NOT VISIBLE if attacker is beyond 15" detection range (13.09).
  7. Unit visible?
    Yes — Apply Benefit of Cover (−1 BS) and Plunging Fire (+1 BS) checks, then target is valid.
    No — Only [INDIRECT FIRE] weapons may still target if in range and eligible; Indirect always grants Benefit of Cover when applicable. Otherwise invalid.
  8. Valid target → Attack sequence — Apply [HEAVY], engaged MON/Veh penalties, and other hit modifiers, then resolve attacks.

Charge Phase — walkthrough (11)

Charges are resolved one declaring unit at a time. A failed charge leaves the unit stationary; a successful charge grants Fights First in the Fight phase.

  1. Start of Charge Phase — Active player may declare charges with eligible units (unengaged, did not Advance or fall back this turn).
  2. Declare Charge — Nominate one unengaged unit within 12" of at least one enemy unit. Select one or more enemy targets within charge distance; all must be reachable if the charge succeeds (11.02).
  3. Charge Roll (2D6) (purple) — Roll 2D6; the result is the maximum distance each model may move toward the targets.
  4. Reach ALL targets? (yellow diamond) — Each model ends closer to charge targets; models that can end within 1" of a target must do so; then must end engaged (2" hor / 5" vert) with every declared target. Cannot engage non-targets.
    Yes — Charge OK: unit moves, ends engaged, gains Fights First. Active player may use Crushing Impact (MON/Veh) just after this charge move (15.06).
    No — Failed: the unit does not move at all; no partial charges.
  5. More units? (yellow diamond)
    Yes — Return to Declare Charge with another unit.
    No — Proceed to end of phase.
  6. End of Charge Phase — Opponent may use Heroic Intervention (charge; gains Fights First if successful) (15.11).

Fight Phase — walkthrough (12)

The Fight phase has four steps: Pile In → Fight → Consolidate, with nested alternation loops during Fight. The same “Resolve Melee Attacks” step is reused in both fight loops.

  1. Start of Fight Phase — Start-of-phase abilities resolve. A unit is eligible to fight if it has not fought yet and: is engaged; was engaged at the start of the Fight step; or made a charge move this turn (12.04).
  2. Pile In (optional, yellow) — Active player resolves pile-in with all chosen eligible units first, then opponent. Each unit may move up to 3" once. Models in base contact cannot move. Must end engaged; engaged models stay engaged with the same enemies (12.02–12.03).
  3. Fights First units remaining? (yellow diamond) — Units with Fights First (chargers, Counter-offensive, etc.) fight before everyone else.
    Yes — Go to alternation step below.
    No — Skip to Remaining Combats check.
  4. Alternate Fights First (yellow) — Players alternate picking one eligible Fights First unit; active player selects first. Each pick: select fight type (Normal or Overrun) → resolve attacks → return to check. If opponent has no eligible Fights First unit, active player continues alone.
  5. Resolve Melee Attacks (red) — Each model: one melee weapon → select up to A engaged targets → gather dice → Hit → Wound → Save → Damage (04, 12.05–12.06). [HAZARDOUS] after unit finishes fighting.
  6. Remaining combats left? (yellow diamond) — Units eligible to fight without Fights First (or already in this pool).
    Yes — Alternate Remaining Combats: player who finished Fights First selects first. After each fight, if new Fights First units became eligible, return to Fights First loop.
    No — All combats resolved; proceed to Consolidation.
  7. Consolidate (optional, yellow) — Active player consolidates all eligible units first, then opponent (units that were eligible to fight this phase). Up to 3"; Ongoing → Engaging → Objective modes in order (12.07–12.08). Engaging consolidation: enemies pulled in that have not fought this phase must be selected to fight immediately.
  8. End of Fight Phase — Turn proceeds to End of Turn step on the Master Loop.

Opponent timing: Counter-offensive (2CP) — non-active player, after an enemy unit finishes fighting: one eligible unit gains Fights First and must be the next unit that player selects to fight (15.12).

End of Turn — walkthrough (07.04)

Unlike most phases, End of Turn is a fixed three-step sequence with no unit loops. It fires once per active player turn, after the Fight phase completes.

  1. Start End of Turn Step — The active player’s turn is almost complete; stratagems and abilities with “end of turn” timing may be used.
  2. 1. General Rules — Resolve any core or army rules that explicitly trigger at end of turn (ongoing effects ending, turn-limited abilities, etc.).
  3. 2. Mission Rules (OC / VP) — Calculate Objective Control and score Victory Points per the mission. Remember: OC is also checked at end of each phase and at end of battle round, not only here (14.04).
  4. 3. Regain Coherency — For each unit that is out of coherency, remove models until coherency is restored. Models removed this way do not trigger “when destroyed” abilities (03.03).
  5. Player Turn Ends — Control passes back to the Master Loop. If the opponent has not yet taken a turn this battle round, they become the active player at Start of Turn Step; otherwise the battle round ends and end-of-round scoring applies.

Core Rules Glossary

TermDefinition
Engagement RangeWithin 2" horizontally and 5" vertically of an enemy model (03.02).
Line of SightCan draw a 1 mm line from any part of one model to any part of another, ignoring models in the firing unit (06.01).
Visible / Not VisibleA unit is Visible if LoS can be drawn and no rule (Obscuring, Hidden, Lone Operative, etc.) makes it Not Visible.
Critical HitUnmodified hit roll of 6. Triggers [SUSTAINED HITS], [LETHAL HITS], etc.
Critical WoundUnmodified wound roll of 6 (or [ANTI-X Y+]). Triggers [DEVASTATING WOUNDS], etc.
Half-strengthInfantry: ≤50% models remaining. Monsters/Vehicles: ≤50% starting Wounds remaining (08.03).
Objective Control (OC)Compare total OC of units within objective range (mission-defined; often 3"). Battle-shocked units contribute OC ‘−’ (14).
SMOKEUnit keyword required for Smokescreen stratagem (15.10).
Set Up / ReinforceUnits that arrive from Reserves or disembark are “set up this turn” — affects [HEAVY], Indirect Fire bands, etc.

Keyword Resolution Order

When multiple rules apply to one attack, resolve in this order: Determine Attacks → Hit Roll (incl. Critical Hit) → Wound Roll (incl. Critical Wound) → Allocate & Save → Inflict Damage → Mortal Wounds → Feel No Pain. Weapon keywords modify the step where they appear in the core rules (04).
